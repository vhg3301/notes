# Internal Audits - Study Notes

*Notes from Google Cybersecurity Course (Day 15)*

# Common elements of internal audits

- Establishing the scope and goals

- Conducting a risk assessment 

- Completing a controls assessment

- Assessing compliance

- Communicating results

# Audit questions

- What is the audit meant to achieve? 

- Which asset are most at risk?

- Are current controls sufficient to protect those assets?

- What controls and compliance regulations need to be implemented?

This questions is important, because they can help you in the next element

# Controls assessment

A controls assessment involves closely review an organization's existing assets
to guarantee if the internal controls and procedures are efficients.

To do this, entry-level analyst can be task with the controls into the following controls

# Control Categories

- Administrative controls

The administrative controls are related to human component of cybersecurity
They include policies and procedures that define how a organization manage his data
Example:
The password implementation policies

- Technical controls

Hardware and software solutions used to protect the assets
Example:
IDS/IPS, firewalls and more

- Physical controls

The implementation of physical defenses to protect digital assets 
Example:
Locks and surveillance camera

# Communication

When a internal audit is completed, result and recomendations need to be communicated to the team.
